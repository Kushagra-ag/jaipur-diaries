History

Flocked by the visitors and tourists throughout the year, Moti Dungri temple of Lord Ganesha, is one of the most significant temple in Jaipur known for its divine powers. Long ago, when Raja of Mewar was travelling in a bullock-cart carrying a massive idol of Ganesh Bhagwan, he decided that he would install the idol at a place where the cart naturally halted. As fate would have it, the cart stopped at the foot of Moti Dungri Hill in Jaipur. This hill also served as a royal residence for Maharaja Sawai Man Singh.

Architecture

The word Moti Dungri means a hill made of pearl because of its striking resemblance to a pearl drop. It is famous for the beautiful shrine of Lord Ganesha that was made by Seth Jai Ram Paliwal in the 18th century (it is said to be more than five hundered years old), who was accompanying Maharaja Madho Singh I, from Udaipur. It was brought to Udaipur from Gujarat. The temple was built under Paliwal's supervision.

A fair is organized at the temple, every Wednesday. The temple is the centre of attraction on festivals like Ganesh Chaturthi, Krishna Janamasthami, Annakut and Paush Bada. Also at Mahashivaratri (the festival of Shiva) there is a lingam in the Moti Dungri complex. Many scholars and architects come here to have flavor of rich cultural heritage of city. Thousands of devotees participate in the preparation of festivities and pay their regards to Lord Ganesha.
