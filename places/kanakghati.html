History

Kanak Ghati was built by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh, just about 280-years ago. It is located at the lowest of Nahargarh slopes in the way to Amber Fort on the amer road and surrounded by the Aravalli hills. The Kanak Vrindavan Valley was built for entertainment and recreation of Maharani and the Maharaja. According to some people the Maharaja planned for an Ashvamedha Yagya for the water from a few streams to collect here. A Lord Krishna temple named Govind Devji was also built around the same time.

The garden consists of artificial waterfalls and ponds, swings and several benches to relax. Playing items for kids are also available here. It has a scene from Hindu mythology where several sculpted statues of girls (Gopiyan) are bathing in a pond, while Lord Krishna is sitting on a tree and stealing clothes of "gopis". The garden has a beautiful fountain system adding to its beauty. The main fountain called 'Parikrama', one of the most popular fountains has been cut from an introspective piece of marble.

It is the favorite picnic destination, and is a place for film shoots, bird watchers and romantic couples. One can come here to spend some quality time because this area is certainly the best for a time, out from the tensed city lifestyles.
